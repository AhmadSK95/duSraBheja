## 2) Project plan for the product he’s suggesting (and how we tailor it to *your* constraints)

What he’s really pitching is a **Second Brain as a system**: one reliable capture behavior from you, and an AI+automation loop that **sorts, routes, stores, and retrieves**… and can grow into **infrastructure** (API-first) instead of staying a personal notes hack. ([Video Highlight | AI Video Summarizer][1])

### A. Product definition (MVP)

**Product:** “Open Brain” (your name can be cooler)
**Promise:** *You drop thoughts anywhere. The system turns them into structured, searchable, reviewable knowledge with minimal effort.*

**Core loop**

1. **Capture** (single habit): message, voice note, meeting transcript, link, snippet
2. **Interpret** (LLM): classify + extract entities + suggest destination + confidence
3. **Route & Store**: write to the right “bucket” (Tasks / Ideas / People / Projects / Notes / Resources)
4. **Review**: when unsure, ask you 1–2 clarifying questions
5. **Retrieve**: chat/search + “build me a view” (dashboards later)

This matches the video’s “architecture > tools” framing. ([Video Highlight | AI Video Summarizer][1])

---

### B. Requirements (what it must do)

**Functional requirements (MVP)**

* Ingest text from at least **one capture surface** (Slack/Discord channel OR a simple web inbox).
* LLM classification into your chosen taxonomy (start small: 5–7 buckets).
* Confidence scoring + a “Needs Review” queue.
* Persist to a storage backend you control (simple first: Postgres or even files; can mirror to Notion/Obsidian).
* Search + retrieval (keyword + basic semantic search).
* Audit trail: original input, model output, final stored record, timestamps.

**Non-functional requirements**

* Privacy: redact sensitive strings (API keys, secrets) on ingest.
* Reliability: retries, dead-letter queue for failures, idempotency keys.
* Cost guardrails: per-day token budget; fall back to cheaper model for routine classification.
* Portability: avoid hard coupling to one SaaS (because “tools aren’t portable”). ([Video Highlight | AI Video Summarizer][1])

---

### C. Architecture options (pick the one that fits your resources)

#### Option 1: “Fast No-Code Spine” (Zapier/n8n + hosted DB)

* Capture: Slack/Discord
* Automation: Zapier or n8n
* LLM: OpenAI or Anthropic
* Storage: Notion DB + mirrored Postgres (or just Notion initially)

Best when: you want speed + iteration.

#### Option 2: “Builder Mode” (your likely sweet spot)

* Capture: Slack/Discord + simple web form
* Backend: FastAPI (Python)
* Queue: Redis queue/Celery or SQS
* Storage: Postgres + pgvector
* LLM: OpenAI (with model routing)
* UI: minimal Next.js or even a CLI to start

Best when: you want **infrastructure**, extensibility, and ownership (very aligned with the “infrastructure not tool” principle). ([Video Highlight | AI Video Summarizer][1])

### D. Phased build plan (tight, shippable)

**Phase 0: Decide your taxonomy + capture habit (1 session)**

* Output: “Brain Contract” doc: buckets, required fields, what goes to review.

**Phase 1: MVP ingestion + classify + store**

* Webhook ingestion (Slack/Discord or web form)
* LLM prompt returning strict JSON: `{type, title, summary, entities, destination, confidence, questions_if_low_conf}`
* Postgres tables + a “needs_review” table

**Phase 2: Review loop (the make-or-break piece)**

* If confidence < threshold → auto-ask you 1–2 clarifying Qs in the same capture surface
* Your answer updates the record and re-runs classification (or just finalizes)

**Phase 3: Retrieval**

* Simple search UI
* “Ask my brain” endpoint (RAG over your stored notes + metadata filters)

**Phase 4: Infrastructure mode**

* Public-ish internal API: `/capture`, `/search`, `/ask`, `/projects/{id}/brief`
* Add “generate a view” later (agent-generated interfaces concept from the video). ([Video Highlight | AI Video Summarizer][1])
